Best Cuban Tres Guitar for Recording (2026)

The Cuban Tres is the soul of son cubano — the rhythmic and melodic backbone of traditional Cuban music. I play a custom-built tres myself, handcrafted to my personal specifications. But if you don't have access to a luthier who can build one for you and you need to buy from a store, online retailers like Thomann offer several quality options. Here are my top picks for studio recording, from the professional choice to the best budget option.

How to Choose the Best Cuban Tres Guitar

The Cuban tres is a unique instrument with a rich history. As a tres player myself, I know that finding the right instrument can be challenging — especially outside Cuba.

Build quality is your top priority. Look for solid wood construction, properly seated frets, a straight neck, and smooth tuning pegs. A well-built tres will stay in tune and project clearly.

Tuning and string gauge matter. The tres is typically tuned G C E with octave pairs. Standard tres string sets are available from D'Addario and La Bella. If you're transitioning from guitar, the narrower string spacing takes some getting used to.

Pickup type determines your amplified sound. Most tres guitars use piezoelectric pickups. Some modern models come with undersaddle or soundhole pickups. If you play amplified regularly, consider a quality pickup system.

My Recommendation: Thomann Tres Cubano Deluxe (No Cutaway)

For professional recordings, I recommend the Deluxe without a cutaway. The solid spruce top gives this tres exceptional clarity, sustain, and definition across all three courses of strings. Without the cutaway, the body produces fuller resonance — critical for capturing that authentic Cuban tone in the studio. The mother of pearl inlays around the sound hole add traditional elegance, and the mahogany neck with acacia fingerboard feels fast and responsive. I record with a tres similar to this, and the solid spruce top is what separates it from budget models. At $469, it's an investment that delivers professional results.

For Live Performance: The Right Pickup Makes All the Difference

A great tres through a bad pickup sounds thin and lifeless. The most important upgrade for live performance is a quality pickup that captures the full body resonance and percussive attack of the tres. These are my top recommendations: K&K Pure Mini — three-sensor system that mounts under the bridge plate for warm, natural tone with zero feedback issues. LR Baggs Anthem — combines a mic and piezo for studio-quality sound on stage. Fishman Rare Earth Blend — magnetic soundhole pickup blended with an internal mic, perfect for cutting through a live mix. If you're installing one yourself, the Fishman Presys Blend offers onboard EQ and dual-source blending at a great price point. A $100–$300 pickup upgrade transforms your tres from a studio-only instrument to a stage-ready powerhouse.
Verdict: Deluxe for the studio, Standard CCW for gigging, K&K Pure Mini for the stage

Final Thoughts

For pure studio tone, the Deluxe without cutaway is unbeatable — the solid spruce top delivers clarity and sustain that no other model matches. If you play lead lines and need upper fret access, step up to the Deluxe CCW. The Standard CCW is the smart choice for gigging musicians, and the Standard Acacia is the best value for beginners. And whichever tres you choose, invest in a quality pickup — the K&K Pure Mini, LR Baggs Anthem, or Fishman Rare Earth Blend will make your instrument stage-ready.
